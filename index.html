About us

Cancer Support Society is a beacon of hope and solidarity in Somalia

Cancer Support Society is a beacon of hope and solidarity in Somalia, founded on the principle that no one should endure the challenges of cancer alone. We are a locally registered non-profit organization dedicated to transforming the landscape of cancer care through awareness, compassion, and collaboration. Our vision is a Somalia where every individual—regardless of socioeconomic status—has equitable access to life-saving treatment, psychological support, and education about cancer prevention and management.

Guided by our mission, we partner with hospitals, communities, and global allies to provide financial aid for treatment, emotional support networks, and nationwide awareness programs. From urban centers to rural villages, we work tirelessly to reduce cancer mortality, empower patients and families, and dismantle the stigma surrounding this disease. Our programs, including the Psychological Support Network, Financial Aid Initiative, and Online Health Guides, reflect our commitment to holistic, inclusive care.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Oasis services, appointments, and patient care policies.

Our hospital offers a comprehensive range of medical services including emergency care, specialized surgical units, cardiology, pediatrics, internal medicine, and diagnostic imaging. We are committed to providing high-quality, patient-centered care across all our departments.

To schedule an appointment, you can call our main line or visit the 'Appointments' section on our website. You may need a referral from your primary care physician, depending on your insurance plan and the specialist you wish to see. Please have your insurance information ready when you call.

General visiting hours are typically from 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM, though this may vary for specific units like ICU or maternity. We ask visitors to check in at the front desk and adhere to all infection control guidelines. Please limit visitors to two at a time per patient.

We accept a wide variety of insurance plans, including most major PPO, HMO, and government plans (like Medicare/Medicaid). To confirm if your specific plan is covered, please contact our billing department directly or check the list on our website before your visit.

For a scheduled admission, please bring your photo ID, insurance card, a list of all current medications (including dosage), any pre-registration paperwork, and minimal personal items like toiletries and comfortable clothing. Please leave valuables at home.
